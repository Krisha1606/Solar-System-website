Quick Facts

  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Distance from the Sun: ~108.2 million km
  • Length of a Day: 243 Earth days (spins very slowly!)
  • Orbit Period: 225 Earth days
  • Moons: 0
  • Surface Temperature: ~465°C (hottest planet)
  • Atmosphere: Thick clouds of CO₂ and sulfuric acid
  • Interesting Fact: Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets!

About Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is often called Earth's "twin" or "sister" because it is similar in size and composition. However, unlike Earth, Venus has no liquid water and is covered by a thick, toxic atmosphere. The atmosphere is made mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid that trap heat — making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system.

At its surface, Venus reaches temperatures of 464°C (867°F) and pressures 92 times that of Earth’s. These extreme conditions turn its lower atmosphere into a supercritical fluid. Venus is also one of the brightest objects in the night sky and is often seen near sunrise or sunset as the “morning star” or “evening star.”

Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets, and its rotation is extremely slow. One Venus day (243 Earth days) is longer than its year (225 Earth days). It has no moons and no rings. Despite its harsh surface, Venus plays a critical role in understanding planetary evolution and climate systems.
