About Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System after Jupiter. It is a gas giant, with an average radius about 9 times that of Earth. Even though Saturn is almost as big as Jupiter, it has only about a third of Jupiter‚Äôs mass.

Its interior is believed to consist of a rocky core, surrounded by layers of metallic hydrogen, liquid hydrogen, and helium, with a gaseous outer layer. Saturn appears pale yellow due to ammonia crystals in its atmosphere, and it has powerful winds reaching up to 1,800 km/h (1,100 mph).
