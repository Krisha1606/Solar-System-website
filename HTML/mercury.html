Quick Facts

  • Type: Rocky Planet
  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Distance from the Sun: ~57.9 million km
  • Length of a Day: 59 Earth days
  • Orbit Period: 88 Earth days
  • Moons: 0
  • Surface Temperature: -180°C (night) to 430°C (day)
  • Atmosphere: Very thin (mainly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen)
  • Interesting Fact: Mercury has the most craters in the solar system!

About Mercury

Mercury's sidereal year (88 Earth days) and sidereal day (58.65 Earth days) are in a 3:2 ratio. This relationship is called spin–orbit resonance. One solar day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts about 176 Earth days. That means one side of Mercury can stay in sunlight or darkness for a full Mercury year. Its surface experiences extreme temperatures, from −170°C at night to 420°C during the day.

Despite being the smallest planet, Mercury's gravity is similar to Mars. Its surface is heavily cratered like Earth's Moon, and it features giant fault scarps called rupes. The largest crater, Caloris Planitia, is 1,550 km wide—one-third the planet’s size! Its poles may contain water ice in permanently shadowed craters. Mercury is always close to the Sun in Earth’s sky, visible at dawn or dusk as a “morning star” or “evening star.”
